# Prime Air — Field Technician Guide

## Welcome

This guide walks you through everything you need to know to use the Prime Air app as a field HVAC technician. The app handles your clock-in, your jobs for the day, checklists, parts, and customer sign-off — all in one place.

---

## Getting Started

### Download the App
- **iPhone:** Search "Prime Air" in the App Store → Tap Install
- **Android:** Search "Prime Air" in Google Play → Tap Install

### Sign In
1. Open the app
2. Tap **Sign In**
3. Enter your cell phone number
4. You'll receive a 6-digit code by text — enter it
5. You're in

If you don't have an account yet, your office manager will send you a text invite link — tap it and follow the steps.

### Allow Permissions (Required)
When asked, tap **Allow** for:
- **Location** → Always (required for GPS time tracking)
- **Camera** → For job photos and clock-in photo
- **Notifications** → For job alerts and reminders

If you accidentally denied any of these, go to your phone's **Settings → Prime Air** and turn them on.

## Your Home Screen

When you open the app you'll see:
- A large **CLOCK IN** button at the top
- Today's **job list** below it
- A **bottom navigation bar** with: Home, Schedule, Time Cards, Chat

## Clocking In & Out

### Clock In
1. Tap the big **CLOCK IN** button
2. Select which **job** you're starting (if you have one assigned)
3. If no job yet, select the **project** you're working on
4. The app takes a quick photo — look at the camera and tap the checkmark
5. Tap **Start** — your timer begins

You may get a notification asking if you want to clock in when you arrive at a job site — tap **Yes** to clock in from the notification without opening the app.

### Switch Jobs Mid-Day
If you move from one job to another:
1. Tap **Switch Job** on the home screen
2. Select the new job
3. Your time on the previous job is automatically saved and a new timer starts

### Taking a Break
1. Tap **Take Break** on the home screen
2. Select break type: **Lunch** or **Rest Break**
3. When you return, tap **End Break**
4. Break time is not counted in your work hours

### Clock Out
1. Tap the **CLOCK OUT** button (same button as Clock In)
2. If asked "Did you take all required breaks?" answer honestly — tap **Yes** or **No**
3. Tap **Confirm** — your timecard is submitted for your supervisor's review

**Important:** Always clock out at the end of your shift. If you forget, contact your dispatcher — they can fix it, but it creates extra work.

## Your Jobs

### View Today's Jobs
From the home screen, scroll down to see all jobs assigned to you today. Each job card shows:
- Job number and customer name
- Address
- Job type (Repair, Maintenance, Installation, etc.)
- Your scheduled time window
- Current status

### Open a Job
Tap any job card to see the full details:
- **What to do:** Scope of work and notes from the dispatcher
- **Equipment:** What unit you're working on (make, model, serial number)
- **Checklist:** Steps to complete
- **Photos:** Any pre-job photos from the office
- **Customer info:** Name, address, phone number

### Start a Job
1. Open the job detail
2. Tap **Navigate** to open Google Maps (or Apple Maps) with directions
3. When you arrive, tap **I'm Here** — this starts your on-site timer and notifies the dispatcher you've arrived
4. The customer receives an automatic text: "Your tech [Your Name] has arrived"

### Complete a Job
Before marking a job complete, make sure:
- [ ] All checklist items are checked off
- [ ] Required photos are uploaded
- [ ] Parts used are recorded
- [ ] Customer has signed (if required)

Then tap **Mark Complete**. The office will generate an invoice automatically.

## Checklists

Every job has a checklist tailored to the job type. For example, an AC Tune-Up checklist includes:

- Check/replace air filter
- Check refrigerant levels
- Check condensate drain
- Check electrical connections
- Check capacitors
- Measure supply/return air temperature difference
- Clean condenser coils
- Test thermostat operation
- (and more...)

### Using the Checklist
1. Open your job → Tap **Checklist**
2. Tap each item as you complete it — it turns green with a checkmark
3. Some items have a notes field — tap the pencil icon to add details
4. You cannot mark the job complete until all required items are checked

---

## Taking Photos

Photos are important — they protect you and document the work.

### Add a Photo to a Job
1. Open the job detail
2. Tap the camera icon or scroll to **Photos** section
3. Tap **Add Photo** → Take a new photo or choose from your camera roll
4. Add a caption (optional but helpful): "Before — dirty coils" or "After — replaced capacitor"
5. Tap **Save** — the photo uploads and is visible to the dispatcher immediately

**Best practice photos:**
- Before starting: equipment condition
- Problem found: what you discovered
- Parts installed: proof of new components
- After completion: system running cleanly
- Any damage found that is NOT part of this job (document it for liability)

---

## Recording Parts Used

If you use any parts from your truck during a job:

1. Open the job → tap **Parts Used**
2. Tap **Add Part**
3. Search for the part by name or part number
4. Enter the quantity used
5. Tap **Save**

This automatically:
- Deducts the part from your truck stock
- Adds the part cost to the job invoice
- Alerts the office when your stock gets low

---

## Completing Forms

Some jobs require you to fill out a form — like a refrigerant tracking log, safety checklist, or equipment inspection report.

1. Open the job → tap **Forms**
2. Tap the form name to open it
3. Fill in each field — some are required (marked with *)
4. For photo fields: tap the camera icon to attach a photo
5. For signature fields: have the customer sign with their finger
6. Tap **Submit** when complete

Submitted forms are stored permanently and can be used for EPA compliance, warranty claims, and customer records.

---

## Customer Sign-Off

For service repairs and installations, you may need the customer's signature:

1. After completing the job, tap **Get Customer Signature**
2. Hand your phone to the customer
3. The customer signs with their finger in the signature box
4. Tap **Done** — the signature is saved to the work order

## Your Schedule

Tap **Schedule** in the bottom navigation to see your upcoming jobs.

- **Today** tab: All jobs scheduled for today in time order
- **Week** tab: All jobs this week in calendar format
- Tap any job to see full details

You'll receive a push notification when a new job is assigned to you or when a job is updated.

---

## Your Time Cards

Tap **Time Cards** in the bottom navigation to see your hours.

- **This Week** tab: All time entries for the current pay period
- Each entry shows: date, project, cost code, hours worked, status
- **Approved** entries have a green checkmark — these are finalized
- **Pending** entries are awaiting supervisor approval

### Viewing Past Timecards
Tap the calendar icon at the top to select a past pay period.

### If Something Looks Wrong
If a timecard entry looks incorrect (wrong hours, wrong project):
1. Tap the entry
2. Tap **Flag Issue** and describe what's wrong
3. Your supervisor will receive an alert and can correct it

**Never** just let wrong hours sit — it affects your paycheck.

---

## Chat

Tap **Chat** in the bottom navigation to message your dispatcher or coworkers.

- **Tap a name** to start a direct message
- **Job threads** appear for each active job — tap to message in context of that specific job
- Tap the **+** icon in a chat to attach a photo or voice memo
- New messages show a red badge on the Chat icon

## Tips & Troubleshooting

### "I forgot to clock in"
Tell your dispatcher. They can manually add a time entry. Always tell them the same day — it's much easier to fix than days later.

### "My app won't connect to the internet"
No problem. The app works offline. Clock in/out, fill out forms, and take photos — everything will sync automatically when you have signal again. You'll see a small "syncing" indicator when it reconnects.

### "My GPS seems off"
Make sure **Location** is set to **Always** in your phone settings for the Prime Air app. If you're in a building with thick walls (like a concrete warehouse), GPS may take a moment to lock on — step outside briefly if needed.

### "I'm at a job but the app doesn't recognize the location"
The office sets up a geofence radius around each job site. If you're parked far away or the address on the work order is slightly off, it may not trigger. Just clock in manually — your dispatcher can verify via the map.

### "My phone died during a shift"
Contact your dispatcher immediately. They can see your last GPS ping and last clock-in and can manually close out your timecard.

### Battery Tips
- The GPS runs in the background while you're clocked in — this does use some battery
- Plug in your phone while driving between jobs
- The app automatically stops GPS tracking when you clock out (it only runs while you're on the clock)

## Privacy

- GPS tracking **only runs while you are clocked in**
- The moment you clock out, all tracking stops completely
- You can view your own GPS history in the app under **Time Cards → [select entry] → View Route**
- Your company can only see your location during your work hours
- Facial photos at clock-in are stored securely and only viewable by your office manager
